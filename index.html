QUALIFICATION & FLIGHT CERTIFICATION REGIME

1. INDEPENDENT ENVIRONMENTAL QUALIFICATION

Research institutes, aerospace companies, and planetary science teams deploy hardware to our facilities for rigorous environmental qualification. We execute controlled test briefs—simulating low pressure limits, high UV flux, abrasive regolith interaction, and communication dropouts—producing structured qualification dossiers and raw sensor telemetry.

Deliverables: Full environmental verification data, instrument calibrations, and structured engineering qualification reports.

2. CO-DEVELOPMENT FLIGHT RESEARCH

In parallel, we research and engineer surface exploration hardware—including low-pressure sensor enclosures, specialized mobility treads, and high-gain antenna arrays—verifying them internally through the same qualification pipeline before final integration.

INFRASTRUCTURE & SIMULATION FACILITIES

Three active facilities operating in sequence to systematically validate space hardware through escalating environmental variables.

FACILITY 1 — MOHALI SIMULATION CHAMBER

MOHALI, PUNJAB | OPERATIONAL YEAR-ROUND

Designed for laboratory-controlled planetary simulation campaigns. Four physical systems operate inside a single integrated vacuum vessel.

VACUUM VESSEL (SM-VAC-01)

  • Chamber Volume: 2.5m × 2m × 2m stainless steel enclosure.
  • Target Pressure: Achievable down to 0.05 atm.
  • Qualification Margin: Replicates low pressure states to qualify mechanical seal integrity, thermal dissipation constraints, and electric motor thermal curves under thin atmosphere.

REGOLITH SIMULANT BED

  • Composition: JSC Mars-1A (NASA standard). Matched to Mars Curiosity surface data.
  • Analytical Validation: Benchmarked in collaboration with IIT Ropar.
  • Qualification Margin: High-abrasion drilling profiles, robotic tread slip coefficients, and sample ingestion testing.

SOLAR ELECTROMAGNETIC SIMULATOR

  • Emitter Array: 8–12 high-intensity UV-B and UV-C lamp arrays.
  • Qualification Margin: Simulates high solar UV flux to verify PCB conformal coating degradation, optical filter refraction changes, and polymer seal integrity.

DEEP SPACE TELEMETRY SIMULATOR

  • Signal Latency: Software-controlled delay scaling from 3 to 20 minutes.
  • Qualification Margin: Mandatory for autonomous flight array verification. Real-time manual overrides fail under simulated latency cycles.

FACILITY 2 — MORNI HILLS MOBILITY RANGE

MORNI HILLS, HARYANA | OPERATIONAL YEAR-ROUND (DAYTIME)

Uneven basalt terrain with natural geological configurations and gradients scaling to 35 degrees. Designed for heavy mobility testing that cannot be simulated under laboratory chamber volumes.

INSTRUMENTATION & TESTING PARAMETERS:

  • Planetary chassis integrity, suspension kinematics, and high-frequency structural vibration under dynamic loads
  • Autonomous flight telemetry and stability in high-shear winds at low air density
  • Spectral mapping, camera calibrations, and LIDAR profiling over natural rocky basalt formations
  • Long-range autonomous navigation loops and power cell degradation cycles scaling to 4 hours

Technical Note: This is an active aerospace test range. Client engineering teams operate payload systems; Stella Martis engineers capture instrumentation logs.

FACILITY 3 — SPITI VALLEY HIGH-ALTITUDE FIELD STATION

SPITI VALLEY, HIMACHAL PRADESH | OPEN JUNE TO SEPTEMBER

Operating at an elevation of 4,400 to 4,800 meters above sea level (ASL), this station provides an atmospheric analog with direct solar radiation and cold desert volcanic geology.

ENVIRONMENTAL CHARACTERISTICS:

  • Atmospheric Pressure: Sustained 0.55 – 0.60 atm
  • Thermal Ranges: 263 K (−10°C) overnight to 291 K (+18°C) midday
  • UV Irradiation: Enhanced solar flux with minimal atmospheric filtration
  • Geomorphology: Active cold desert, basalt-dominated geology, and absolute vegetation-free profiles
  • Flight Corridor: 1.5 km unobstructed flight corridor for high-altitude drone research

SPITI QUALIFICATION EXPEDITIONS

Sustained two-week environmental campaigns. Technical teams deploy to the station to run hardware through diurnal cycles. We manage logistics, monitor telemetry, and supply engineering dossiers. Scientific co-authorship available for research findings.

Qualification Budget: Campaigns scale from ₹5L to ₹10L based on payload dimensions and telemetry profiles.
