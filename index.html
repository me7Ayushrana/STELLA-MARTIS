MARS ANALOG
TESTING INFRASTRUCTURE
— INDIA

Stella Martis builds and operates environments that replicate conditions on the Martian surface. Space hardware — rovers, drones, sensors, communication systems, autonomy software — is tested here before deployment.

We operate three facilities in northern India. One indoor laboratory outside Chandigarh. One outdoor terrain range in Morni Hills. One high-altitude field station in Spiti Valley. We also design and build Mars-ready hardware of our own, tested through the same pipeline.

WHAT WE DO

1. WE TEST OTHER PEOPLE'S HARDWARE

Research institutions, aerospace companies, university programs, and government agencies bring their technology to our facilities. We run it through controlled simulations — low pressure, UV radiation, regolith interaction, signal delay — and document what happens.

Outcome: Clients leave with test data and a technical report.

2. WE BUILD OUR OWN HARDWARE

We design Mars surface technology — sensors, enclosures, mobility systems, communication tools — and validate them in our own facilities before offering them for mission use.

INFRASTRUCTURE & FACILITIES

Three facilities. Used in sequence or independently depending on what you need to test.

FACILITY 1 — INDOOR LABORATORY

MOHALI, PUNJAB | OPEN YEAR-ROUND

This is where most test campaigns begin. Four systems operate inside a single controlled environment.

VACUUM CHAMBER

  • Size: 2.5m × 2m × 2m internal volume.
  • Pressure: Achievable down to 0.05 atm.
  • Utility: Mars atmospheric pressure is 0.006 atm. We reach ~0.05–0.10 atm — sufficient to test seal integrity, pressure ratings, motor behaviour in thin atmosphere, and thermal dissipation.

REGOLITH BED

  • Mass: 200 kg floor bed of Mars soil simulant.
  • Composition: JSC Mars-1A (NASA standard). Matched to Curiosity rover surface data. Validated with IIT Ropar.
  • Utility: Testing rover treads, drill bits, landing legs, and sampling mechanisms.

UV ARRAY

  • Hardware: 8–12 UV-B and UV-C lamps covering chamber floor.
  • Utility: Replicates Mars's ~100x Earth UV flux for material testing (PCB coatings, seals, lenses, solar panels).

COMMUNICATIONS DELAY SIMULATOR

  • Latency: Programmable 3 to 20 minutes.
  • Utility: Tests autonomy. If your system requires real-time human intervention to function, it will fail here.

FACILITY 2 — OUTDOOR TERRAIN RANGE

MORNI HILLS, HARYANA | OPEN YEAR-ROUND, DAYTIME

Rocky, uneven terrain with natural basalt-rich geology and gradients up to 35 degrees. Used for mobility testing that cannot be conducted indoors.

TESTING CAPABILITIES:

  • Rover chassis performance on loose and compacted terrain
  • Drone flight stability and terrain-following at moderate altitude
  • Camera and LIDAR mapping over natural surface geology
  • Untethered autonomy runs up to 4 hours

Note: This is a working test range, not a demonstration site. Client teams operate their own hardware. Our engineers log instrumentation data.

FACILITY 3 — SPITI VALLEY FIELD STATION

SPITI VALLEY, HIMACHAL PRADESH | OPEN JUNE TO SEPTEMBER

Elevation: 4,400 – 4,800 metres ASL. A deployable outpost acting as base infrastructure (converted shipping container) for prep and workspace.

WHY SPITI:

  • Pressure: Natural atmospheric pressure at altitude: 0.55 – 0.60 atm
  • Temperature: −10°C overnight to +18°C midday during season
  • UV Exposure: 8–10 hours daily with minimal atmospheric filtration
  • Terrain: Cold desert, volcanic geology, no vegetation
  • Flight: Drone corridor: 1.5 km unobstructed flight range

THE SPITI EXPEDITION

Our two-week field campaign. Client team travels to site. Hardware runs through daily test cycles. Data is logged, documented, and compiled into a technical report. Research paper co-authorship is available.

Pricing: ₹5L – ₹10L depending on team size and equipment.

HOW TESTING WORKS

STEP 1

SUBMIT A TEST BRIEF

Tell us what you're testing, what conditions matter, and what data you need. Use the form on our contact page or email us directly.

STEP 2

WE SCOPE THE CAMPAIGN

We review your hardware specs and define which facilities and systems apply. We send back a campaign plan with timeline, cost, and deliverables.

STEP 3

CAMPAIGN EXECUTION

Your team brings hardware to our facility. Our engineers set up and monitor all simulation systems. You operate your hardware. We log everything.

STEP 4

DATA AND REPORT

You receive raw test data and a structured technical report. If publishable findings emerge, we discuss co-authorship.

RESEARCH & COLLABORATION

Stella Martis maintains a research program parallel to its testing operations. We publish findings from both client campaigns (with permission) and our own hardware development work.

CURRENT RESEARCH AREAS:

  • Mars regolith simulant composition and fidelity benchmarking
  • Electronics performance characterisation in sub-0.1 atm environments
  • Autonomous navigation under communication delay constraints
  • Hardware degradation under combined UV and thermal cycling conditions

Academic Partners: IIT Ropar (geology validation). We are open to research collaborations with universities, ISRO centres, and independent research groups.
